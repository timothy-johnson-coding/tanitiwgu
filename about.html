About Taniti

A tropical Pacific island where rainforest meets reef, and tradition meets a warm island welcome.

The island

Where in the world is Taniti?

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the South Pacific, covering less than 500 square miles. Despite its size, the island is remarkably varied: golden and rocky beaches ring the coast, a deep, safe harbor shelters the capital, and the interior rises through dense rainforest to a mountainous spine.

At the island's heart stands Mount Pulu, a small but active volcano that is carefully monitored and a highlight for adventurous visitors. The surrounding lowlands are blanketed in lush jungle alive with birdsong and tropical flowers.

  • Less than 500 square miles of land
  • Sandy beaches, rocky coves & a sheltered harbor
  • Tropical rainforest and a mountainous interior
  • One small, monitored active volcano — Mount Pulu
The people

Meet the Mereans

Taniti is home to roughly 20,000 residents, most of them indigenous Mereans whose ancestors have lived on the island for centuries. Family, community and respect for the land sit at the centre of island life.

Visitors are welcomed warmly. You'll find that a smile and a friendly "Mauri!" (hello) open doors everywhere — from the harbor markets of Yasawa to the smallest village guesthouse.

Tourism is the island's most important industry, and Tanitians take genuine pride in sharing their home, food and traditions with guests from around the world.

Good to know

Visitor essentials

Everything you need to feel at home from the moment you arrive.

🗣️

Language

Merean is the native language, but English is widely spoken across the island, especially in hospitality and tourism.

💵

Currency

The Taniti Dollar (T$) is local currency. Major cards are accepted at hotels and larger venues; carry cash for markets and villages.

🏥

Health & safety

Taniti is very safe. Taniti General Hospital in Yasawa has modern facilities and multilingual staff available 24/7.

🌡️

Climate

Tropical and warm year-round (around 80–85°F). Drier season runs May–October; green season brings lush scenery.

🔌

Power & connectivity

Standard outlets and reliable mobile coverage in populated areas. Wi-Fi is available in most hotels and cafés.

🕐

Time zone

Taniti runs on island time (UTC+11). See the current local time in the bar at the top of every page.
