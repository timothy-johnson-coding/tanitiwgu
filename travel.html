Getting here & around

Everything you need to reach Taniti and explore the island once you arrive.

Getting here

Flying to Taniti

All visitors arrive through Taniti International Airport (TAN), a short drive from the capital, Yasawa. Regional carriers operate direct and one-stop flights from major Pacific hubs.

  • ✈️
    From Honolulu (HNL)~6 hrs · several flights weekly
  • ✈️
    From Sydney (SYD)~5 hrs · daily connections
  • ✈️
    From Tokyo (NRT)~8 hrs · via regional hub
  • ⚓
    By seaSeasonal cruise ships and inter-island ferries dock at Yasawa Harbor
Before you fly

Entry essentials

🛂

Passport

Valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates.

📄

Visa

Visa-free stays up to 30 days for most visitors; longer stays need a permit.

💉

Health

No special vaccinations required. Bring sunscreen, insect repellent and any personal medication.

🧳

Customs

Declare fresh food and plant material to protect the island's ecosystem.

Getting around

Exploring the island

Taniti is small and easy to navigate. Distances are short, the pace is relaxed, and most journeys are scenic.

🚌

Island bus

An affordable public bus loops between Yasawa, the Coral Coast and major trailheads. Pay the driver in cash; service slows in the evenings.

🚕

Taxis

Metered taxis wait at the airport and harbor. Agree fares for longer trips. A handy choice for evenings and groups.

🛵

Scooter & bike rental

The most popular way to explore independently. Rent by the day in Yasawa — bring an international driving permit.

🚗

Car hire

A small number of rental cars are available; book ahead. Roads are quiet but can be narrow and winding inland.

⛴️

Water taxis

Small boats shuttle to coves and snorkel spots not easily reached by road — arrange at the harbor.

🚶

On foot

Yasawa is compact and walkable, with the market, restaurants and harbor all within easy strolling distance.
